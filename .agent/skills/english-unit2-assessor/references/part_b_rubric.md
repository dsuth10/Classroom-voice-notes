# Year 5 English (Unit 2) — Part B Writing & Creating Rubric

This reference file contains the official marking criteria and achievement standards (from A to E) used to assess the written natural disaster information reports.

---

## 📊 Marking Criteria Grid

### A Standard (High / Excellent)
*   **Text Structure & Cohesion:**
    *   Creates a detailed and highly cohesive written and/or multimodal informative report for a specific audience.
    *   Develops and expands on researched ideas with rich supporting details, logically organised into paragraphs.
    *   Draws information from multiple highly authoritative, verified sources.
    *   Uses subheadings, bullet points, and text connectives to seamlessly guide the reader.
*   **Language & Vocabulary:**
    *   Combines sophisticated language features including objective scientific voice, expanded noun groups/phrases, and a varied range of sentence types (simple, compound, complex).
    *   Uses precise, topic-specific technical vocabulary (e.g., *catchment, structural integrity, meteorological conditions, mitigation*).
    *   Exhibits flawless capitalisation of proper nouns and highly consistent tense control.

### B Standard (Above Standard)
*   **Text Structure & Cohesion:**
    *   Creates a written and/or multimodal informative report for an audience.
    *   Develops and expands on ideas with clear supporting details organised into paragraphs.
    *   Draws information from reliable sources.
    *   Uses paragraphs and the starting points of sentences to logically sequence ideas and guide the reader.
*   **Language & Vocabulary:**
    *   Selects and uses language features including objective language, expanded noun groups, complex sentences, and tenses.
    *   Uses precise and topic-specific vocabulary suited to the natural disaster topic.

### C Standard (At Standard)
*   **Text Structure & Cohesion:**
    *   Creates a written and/or multimodal informative report for an audience, developing and expanding on ideas with supporting details from topics or texts studied.
    *   Uses well-defined paragraphs to organise and link ideas.
*   **Language & Vocabulary:**
    *   Uses a range of sentence types including compound and complex sentences.
    *   Uses correct tenses and topic-specific vocabulary.
    *   Maintains basic capitalisation and spacing accuracy (with minor errors).

### D Standard (Below Standard)
*   **Text Structure & Cohesion:**
    *   Creates a written informative report by developing simple ideas about the topic.
    *   Organises text into paragraphs, but paragraphs may lack clear topic sentences or logical connections.
*   **Language & Vocabulary:**
    *   Uses simple and compound sentences, with minimal use of complex structures.
    *   Uses basic topic vocabulary.
    *   Contains frequent spacing, spelling, and punctuation flow errors.

### E Standard (Emerging)
*   **Text Structure & Cohesion:**
    *   Creates a basic written report with limited structural sequencing or paragraph logic.
*   **Language & Vocabulary:**
    *   Relies primarily on simple sentences and conversational vocabulary.
    *   Frequent structural, spelling, and grammar errors that disrupt reading.
