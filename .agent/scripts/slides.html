Plant Germination Experiment

Investigating how different conditions affect seed growth

1. Start with a Question

What do we want to find out?

Good scientists always start their investigations with a clear inquiry question.

Your question should tell us exactly what you are investigating.

  • Example: "How does the amount of sunlight affect germination?"
  • Example: "Will a seed germinate faster in soil or cotton wool?"

2. Make a Hypothesis

What is a Hypothesis?

A hypothesis is your prediction of what will happen in the experiment.

We write it as: "I think that... because..."

  • Example: "I think that the seed in the sunlight will germinate faster because plants need sunlight for energy."

It's okay if your hypothesis is wrong! That's how we learn.

3. Planning a Fair Test

Why do we need a Fair Test?

To know exactly what caused a change, we must make sure our experiment is fair.

  • CHANGE only ONE thing (e.g., amount of water).
  • MEASURE ONE thing (e.g., how tall the plant grows).
  • KEEP THE SAME everything else! (e.g., same seed, same cup, same dirt).

Our Setup

What you will need:

  • A growing container (like a cup)
  • Your chosen growing medium (soil, etc.)
  • Your seeds
  • Water
  • Your student planner document

We will set these up after planning your procedure.

4. Write the Procedure

Step-by-Step Instructions

Your procedure is the list of instructions for your experiment. It must be very clear!

  1. Use numbered steps (1, 2, 3...)
  2. Start each step with an action word (Put, Pour, Measure, Add)
  3. Include specific amounts (e.g., "Pour 50mL of water")
